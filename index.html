Interview Section


1) If you have been invited for an interview the employer is already impressed by your resume. Now, you have to verify that you are the worker described in the resume.

2) First impressions count. Interviewers will look at how you are dressed, your manners,how confident your handshake is eye contact.

3) Soft skills are not on your resume.They will analyze your critical thinking ability, thought process,and your ability to handle difficult situations.

4) DO NOT MENTION A WEAKNESS THAT IS PIVOTAL TO THE JOB FUNCTION.
FOR EXAMPLE, IF IT IS A CUSTOMER SERVICE ROLE, DO NOT MENTION THAT DEALING WITH CUSTOMERS IS A GROWTH AREA FOR YOU.

5) Preparation for the interview. Make sure you understand the job and the company to the best of your ability.

6) Ask questions after the Interview.

Apply the S.T.A.R method:


Situation: Describe the situation that you were in or the task that you needed to accomplish. You must describe a specific event or situation, and refer back to the job description.

Task: What goal were you working toward?

Action: Describe the actions you took to address the situation with an appropriate amount of detail and keep the focus on YOU.

Result: Describe the outcome of your actions and don’t be shy about taking credit for your behavior. What happened? How did the event end? What did you accomplish? What did you learn?

Subject-Verb Agreement Section


1)When figuring out subject verb agreement, both the verb and the subject have to agree with each other.

2)If the subject of a sentence is plural, then the verb of that sentence is plural.

3)If the subject of the sentence is singular then the verb of the sentence is singular.

4)The meaning of what's being expressed in a sentence can determined if the verb that’s needed should be in plural or singular form. These can also refer to collective nouns as well.

5)Subject Verb agreement also deals with Singular/Plural Indefinite/Collective Nouns and Inverted subjects.
